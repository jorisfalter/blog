Why Work and Life should be not too damn serious!

In his foreword for the Navalmanack, Tim Ferriss describes Naval Ravikant as someone who "doesn't take himself too damn seriously".


I believe that's an attitude we can all use, both in life and in work.


Hence a list of things which are "not to damn serious":

  • It wasn't intended that way, but boxy Kei Cars are fun
  • Early 2021, the Reddit Wallstreetbets group decided to make fun of companies short-selling, by pumping the price of specific stocks to a tenfold and more. In early 2022 the price still floates around ...
  • Similar to the above, around the same time Reddit users started to pump Dogecoin.
  • A landmark video, DollarShaveClub definitely launched not too damn serious
  • Your boss should be fun. I'm lucky mine is.
  • In 2016 the British Natural Environment Research Council launched a poll to name their new boat, the poll got hijacked by a radio station, resulting in a not too damn serious name!
  • In Montreux, Switzerland, Nestle put a big fork in the lake. I think this could be improved upon. Brussels could put a big Brussels Sprout on their central square. Rome a massive pizza.
  • Elon Musk build the CyberTruck. Elon in general doesn't take himself too damn serious.
  • When Product Hunt gives an error page, you can choose to see just more pets
